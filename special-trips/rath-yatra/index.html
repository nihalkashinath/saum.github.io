Detailed Itinerary

Day 1 · July 15: Bhubaneswar Temples, Raghurajpur & Puri

Morning

Arrive at Bhubaneswar Airport 📍

Arrive in Bhubaneswar in the early morning. Private transfer awaits you at the airport — your journey into Odisha's sacred heart begins.

Morning

Lingaraj Temple 📍

One of the oldest and largest temples in Bhubaneswar, dedicated to Harihara (a combined form of Shiva and Vishnu). An extraordinary specimen of Kalinga-style temple architecture, dating back to the 11th century.

Morning

Mukteshwar Temple 📍

A jewel of early Odishan temple art, renowned for its intricately carved torana (gateway) and detailed sculptural panels. Often described as a masterpiece of Kalinga architecture.

Morning

Rajarani Temple 📍

A 11th-century temple celebrated for its remarkable sculptures of apsaras and dikpalas (guardians of the eight directions). Known as the "love temple" of Bhubaneswar for its exquisite carvings.

Afternoon

Lunch

A leisurely vegetarian lunch before the drive to Puri.

Afternoon

Raghurajpur Art Village (en route) 📍

A heritage walk through Odisha's most celebrated craft village, home to master Pattachitra painters, stone carvers, and traditional artisans. Every home here is also a studio — a deeply moving encounter with living tradition.

Evening

Check-in — Premium Beachside Hotel, Puri 📍

Settle in at a premium 3-star beachside hotel in Puri. Fresh up and prepare for an evening of devotion.

Evening

Exclusive Mahaprasad Arrangement 📍

Receive the sacred Mahaprasad of Lord Jagannath, exclusively arranged for you. Considered among the holiest blessings a devotee can receive, the Mahaprasad is prepared in the world's largest temple kitchen and carries Lord Jagannath's divine grace.

Day 2 · July 16: Rath Yatra VIP Experience

Early AM

Transfer to VIP Viewing Area 📍

Early morning transfers by auto to the exclusive VIP area near the Jagannath Puri Mandir — positioned for an unobstructed and intimate view of the grand chariot procession.

Morning

Breakfast at the VIP Corridor

A nourishing vegetarian breakfast served at the VIP corridor, so you can settle in and witness the morning rituals and preparations before the chariots are pulled.

All Day

Rath Yatra — The Grand Chariot Procession 📍

Witness Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra emerge from the Jagannath Temple on their magnificent chariots — Nandighosh, Taladhwaja, and Darpadalana — as millions of devotees gather to pull them towards Gundicha Temple. Comfort arrangements, dedicated crowd management support, unlimited tea, coffee, and water are provided throughout the day.

Afternoon

Special Lunch

A special vegetarian lunch served at the VIP area, so you need not miss a moment of the proceedings.

Evening

Evening High Tea & Return to Hotel

High tea at the VIP corridor followed by auto transfers back to the hotel. Buffet dinner and overnight rest.

Day 3 · July 17: Gundicha Temple, Jagannath Parikrama & Mela

Morning

Breakfast at Hotel

A relaxed breakfast before a morning of further darshan.

Morning

Gundicha Temple 📍

Visit the Gundicha Temple — the "garden house" of Lord Jagannath, where the deities reside for 9 days during the Rath Yatra period. This is considered one of the most auspicious times to visit, as the Lord is believed to be more accessible to devotees here.

Morning

Jagannath Temple Outer Parikrama 📍

Subject to accessibility

Walk the outer Parikrama (circumambulation path) of the great Jagannath Temple — one of the four sacred Dhams of India — soaking in the sacred energy of the precinct.

Afternoon

Rath Yatra Mela 📍

Explore the vibrant Rath Yatra fair — a colourful world of Odishan handicrafts, Pattachitra art, stone carvings, local delicacies, and festive offerings. A wonderful opportunity to take home a piece of Puri's sacred culture.

Afternoon

Buffet Lunch at Hotel & Leisure

Buffet lunch back at the hotel, followed by free time for personal shopping, rest, or a quiet walk by the beach.

Day 4 · July 18: Konark, Chandrabhaga & Departure

Morning

Breakfast & Checkout

Breakfast at the hotel. Checkout by 9 AM and prepare for the scenic return journey.

Morning

Konark Sun Temple 📍

A visit to the magnificent 13th-century Konark Sun Temple — a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Conceived as a colossal chariot of the sun god Surya, with 24 intricately carved stone wheels and seven horses, it is one of India's greatest architectural marvels.

Mid-morning

Chandrabhaga Beach 📍

A quiet moment at Chandrabhaga Beach — serene, unspoiled, and considered sacred for its association with sun worship. A peaceful close to a deeply spiritual journey.

Afternoon

Drop-off at Bhubaneswar Airport

Drive back to Bhubaneswar Airport via the scenic marine route. Depart with hearts full of Lord Jagannath's blessings.

Recommended Preparations

What to Wear
  • Traditional Indian clothing is recommended — kurta-pyjama or dhoti for men, saree or salwar-kameez for women
  • Comfortable slip-on footwear for easy removal at temple entrances
  • Light cotton fabrics — Puri in July is warm and humid
  • Modest, respectful clothing throughout — especially important at Lingaraj Temple and the Jagannath precinct
What to Bring
  • Small bag for personal offerings and daily essentials
  • Umbrella or light raincoat — July is peak monsoon season in Odisha
  • Cap and sunscreen for outdoor stretches
  • Comfortable walking shoes for heritage walks and the Mela
  • Any personal medications, especially if travelling with elderly family members
Personal Offerings
  • Cash in small denominations for donations and the Mela
  • Tulsi leaves and fresh flowers — traditional offerings to Lord Jagannath
  • Sacred threads (mauli) for personal prayers
  • Your heartfelt sankalp (intention) — Lord Jagannath is believed to grant the deepest wishes of sincere devotees
Mantras & Prayers
  • "Jai Jagannath" — the universal chant of the festival, heard millions of times on Rath Yatra day
  • "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya" — the primary Vishnu mantra
  • "Shri Jagannath Ashtakam" — a devotional hymn to Lord Jagannath
  • Prepare a personal sankalp to present at Gundicha Temple, where the Lord is most accessible
Managing the Crowds
  • Rath Yatra draws millions of devotees — stay close to your group and tour manager at all times
  • Our VIP viewing arrangement keeps you away from the densest crowd areas
  • Follow guide instructions for safe and smooth transitions between locations
  • Elderly travellers will receive dedicated personal assistance throughout — please inform us of any specific needs at the time of booking

Frequently Asked Questions

Travel & Timing
Q: What are the exact travel dates?
A: The trip runs from July 15–18, 2026 (4 days). The Rath Yatra itself falls on July 16, 2026.
Q: Where does the trip start and end?
A: The trip begins and ends at Bhubaneswar Airport. You will need to arrange your own travel to/from Bhubaneswar — we can facilitate flight and train bookings if required.
Q: What if there are flight delays?
A: Our team is on the ground and will adjust where possible to accommodate delays. Please keep us informed of any changes to your travel schedule.
Accommodation & Meals
Q: What standard of hotel accommodation is provided?
A: A premium 3-star beachside hotel in Puri for 3 nights. The hotel is selected for comfort, cleanliness, and convenient access to the Rath Yatra route.
Q: Are all meals vegetarian?
A: Yes, all included meals are pure vegetarian, in keeping with the sanctity of the pilgrimage.
Q: Can dietary restrictions be accommodated?
A: Yes, please inform us at the time of booking. Jain food and other specific requirements can be arranged in advance.
Transportation & Group
Q: What kind of transportation is provided?
A: Private AC vehicles for all airport transfers and inter-city travel. On Rath Yatra day, auto transfers are used to navigate through the festival traffic to reach the VIP area.
Q: Will there be a tour guide?
A: Yes, a dedicated professional tour manager accompanies the group for all 4 days, along with expert local guides for temple tours and the heritage walk.
Pricing & Payments
Q: What is not included in the package price?
A: Airfare or train tickets to/from Bhubaneswar; personal expenses and shopping; meals or activities not listed in the itinerary; any costs arising from delays or accessibility changes due to crowd or security conditions.
Q: What is your cancellation policy?
A: Bookings once made cannot be cancelled or refunded, but you may transfer your booking to another person. Any costs for re-issuing transport tickets under a different name will be charged at actuals.
Q: Do you accept installment payments?
A: Given limited seats and high demand, full payment at the time of booking is required to confirm your place.
Spiritual Experience
Q: Can non-Hindus participate in this trip?
A: Non-Hindus are welcome to witness the Rath Yatra procession — it takes place on public streets and is open to all. However, entry inside the Jagannath Temple and Gundicha Temple is traditionally restricted to Hindus. Our guides will ensure everyone has a meaningful, respectful experience.
Q: Is this suitable for elderly travellers?
A: Yes — the itinerary is specifically designed to be senior citizen friendly with a comfort-first pace, dedicated support, crowd management assistance, and private transport. However, many locations are not wheelchair accessible, so we regretfully will not be able to accommodate wheelchair users. Please inform us of any mobility or health requirements at the time of booking so we can make appropriate arrangements.
Q: What if I am not familiar with Hindu traditions?
A: No prior knowledge is required. Our guides will explain the significance of each ritual, location, and tradition throughout the journey, helping you participate respectfully and meaningfully.
